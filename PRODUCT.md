# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design recruiters, creative directors, and potential clients evaluating Sakah Vernyuy Mercy's product design and UX research capability. They arrive from portfolios, referrals, job applications, or Dribbble/Behance links. Their context is a brief, competitive scan — they need to assess design maturity, strategic thinking, and craft quality within seconds.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that communicates professional-level product design competence. Not a gallery of deliverables, but a showcase of design thinking: research-driven process, strategic decision-making, and meticulous craft. The site itself is the strongest portfolio piece — it must demonstrate the quality it claims.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, understated. Three words: **rigorous, warm-authoritative, meticulous**.

The tone is that of a senior design leader presenting to stakeholders: clear, direct, no defensiveness, no buzzwords. The aesthetic is premium-dark — not because "tools look cool dark," but because a deep, controlled surface creates the focus and seriousness the work deserves.

## Anti-references

- Generic portfolio layouts (centered hero, three identical project cards, skill bars with percentages)
- Template-looking sections (the "About Me with avatar + paragraph" cliché)
- Student portfolio aesthetics (excessive gradients, loud typography, over-engineered hover states)
- AI-generated design slop: gradient text, glassmorphism, side-stripe borders, round-everything cards
- SaaS-y metric blocks ("5+ Years Experience" as a big number with no context)

## Design Principles

1. **The site IS the portfolio piece.** Every layout decision, interaction, and pixel demonstrates the design maturity the copy describes. If a section looks generic, it undermines the claim.
2. **Research before artifact.** Process and thinking matter more than final screens. Show how decisions were made, not just what was made.
3. **Edit ruthlessly.** One decisive visual move beats five hesitant ones. Fewer sections, stronger execution. No filler.
4. **Dark as focus, not as trend.** The dark theme serves the content — it creates a controlled, gallery-like environment where the work leads. Warm off-black backgrounds, not gamer-black.
5. **Tension and release.** Tight typographic spacing opens into generous section gaps. Intentional asymmetry breaks the grid for emphasis. Not every element is centered; not every section is symmetrical.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast minimum (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text)
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: collapse all transform-based motion, keep opacity fades at 200ms max
- Respect `prefers-color-scheme`: dark mode only (portfolio choice, not system-dependent)
- Interactive elements must be keyboard-navigable and have visible focus rings
- Form inputs in contact section: proper labels, error states, ARIA attributes
